## Design Context

### Users
People browsing longform articles in Chrome or Edge who want a quick, reasonably accurate word count and lightweight progress tracking without leaving the page.

### Brand Personality
Calm, editorial, precise. The interface should feel trustworthy, light, and quietly polished instead of flashy or overly technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the current soft editorial light-mode feel: airy spacing, pastel-tinted backgrounds, rounded controls, and concise utility-focused surfaces. Changes should make the popup denser without making it feel cramped or mechanical.

### Design Principles
- Surface the core answer immediately.
- Compress controls through better information density, not visual crowding.
- Keep metadata glanceable and secondary to the main count.
- Preserve soft contrast, rounded forms, and a calm reading-oriented tone.
- Favor accessible, explicit semantics even when visible labels are shortened.
